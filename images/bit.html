        Bitcoin hits record high; passes $60,000 for first time

        AFP | Updated: Mar 13, 2021, 19:20 IST

        TimesPoints

        80
        0 Added

        Visit TOI daily & earn TimesPoints!

        PARIS: Bitcoin briefly rose above $60,000 for the first time on Saturday, as increasing backing from corporate heavyweights helps the world's most popular virtual currency continue its record-breaking run.
        The cryptocurrency hit an all-time high of $60,012 at 1149 GMT, according to the website CoinMarketCap.
        Bitcoin has been on a meteoric rise since March last year, when it stood at $5,000, spurred by online payments giant PayPal saying it would allow account holders to use cryptocurrency.
        Last month Elon Musk's electric carmaker Tesla invested $1.5 billion in the virtual unit, while Twitter chief Jack Dorsey and rap mogul Jay-Z said they are creating a fund aimed at making Bitcoin "the internet's currency".
        Others jumping on the bandwagon include Wall Street player BNY Mellon, investment fund giant BlackRock and credit card titan Mastercard.
        Bitcoin, which was launched back in 2009, hit the headlines in 2017 after soaring from less than $1,000 in January to almost $20,000 in December of the same year.
        The virtual bubble then burst in subsequent days, with bitcoin's value then fluctuating wildly before sinking below $5,000 by October 2018.
        However the last year's rise has been more steady, with investors and Wall Street finance giants wooed by dizzying growth, the opportunity for profit and asset diversification, and a safe store of value to guard against inflation.
        Bitcoins are traded via a decentralised registry system known as a blockchain.
        The system requires massive computer processing power in order to manage and implement transactions.
        That power is provided by "miners", who do so in the hope they will receive new bitcoins for validating transaction data.

        Boeing unveils order for troubled 737 MAX, shares jump

        Reuters | Mar 13, 2021, 17:11 IST

        TimesPoints

        80
        0 Added

        Visit TOI daily & earn TimesPoints!

        A Boeing 737 MAX airplane lands after a test flight at Boeing Field in Seattle. (File photo)
        NEW DELHI: Boeing Co unveiled a new order for its 737 MAX on Friday, pushing its shares up 6% as it renews efforts to recapture investor confidence following a two-year safety crisis.
        The deal to sell 24 of the 737-8 model to a backer of Canadian low-cost carrier Flair Airlines comes after Reuters reported it was poised to win another, much larger deal with Southwest Airlines.
        Shares in Boeing rose 6.2% to $267.86.
        Boeing has been trying to rebuild its image with passengers and airlines following the nearly two-year grounding of the MAX after crashes in Indonesia and Ethiopia killed 346 people.
        This week marked the second anniversary of the second accident, with a final investigative report expected any day.
        Boeing said Miami-based private equity firm 777 Partners, which has a stake in Flair Airlines, agreed to buy 24 737-8 airplanes with an option to purchase a further 60.
        Flair, which was recently relaunched by veterans of European budget giant Wizz Air, is now operating one plane for domestic flights. It said it would lease 13 of the 24 aircraft from 777 Partners starting this year.
        Reuters on Wednesday reported Boeing was on the verge of a deal to sell dozens of 737 MAX 7 jets to Southwest Airlines in potentially its largest 737 MAX order since the jet’s safety ban was lifted.
        Both deals would provide a much-needed injection of cash for the US planemaker, which ended last year with more than $60 billion in debt and an historic loss of $12 billion.
        The coronavirus pandemic has further complicated the MAX’s recovery by decimating demand for air travel and new jets.
        Ultra low-cost carriers, or ULCCs, are seen as the winners of the Covid-19 crisis as they offer a no-frills experience at low prices.
        They are pervasive in Europe’s fragmented market, with Hungarian Wizz Air - a key Airbus customer - competing with the likes of Ireland’s Ryanair, a top Boeing user like Southwest.
        They have grown more slowly in North America.
        In December, Alaska Airlines agreed in December to buy 23 737 MAX 9 jets and Ryanair ordered 75 jets.
        At Southwest, Boeing was fending off a challenge from the newer Airbus A220, building on a position as the airline’s exclusive supplier as Airbus struggles to reduce production costs for the Canadian-designed A220, industry sources said.
        Analysts have described Southwest as a must-win for Boeing and crucial to its broader recovery.
        Analysts caution it faces a list of other challenges, from production of its 787 Dreamliner to the delayed development of its 777X and financial overruns on a US Air Force tanker.

        Fulfil the funding dream for your startup, here’s how

        SPOTLIGHT | Mar 11, 2021, 02:07 IST

        TimesPoints

        80
        0 Added

        Visit TOI daily & earn TimesPoints!

        India added more than 1,600 tech startups in 2020, making it the third-largest tech startup hub in the world, according to Nasscom’s Strategic Review 2021 titled ‘New World: The Future is Virtual’. This is the highest addition in three years, taking the total number of tech startups to 12,500, including 12 new unicorns. The country roughly has about 55,000 startups. It’s thus clear that even the pandemic hasn’t dampened the startup spirit of India.
        But, while the ecosystem has evolved and come a long way, particularly in terms of funding, it’s still at an adolescent stage compared to China and the US. It’s still very tricky for a startup to find an initial investment for giving its idea a shape. Most founders rely on friends and family for the initial round of funding. This, despite a much stronger VC community in place today. So, what are the best ways to fund a startup? What is still lacking in the ecosystem?
        “Depending on the stage of startups, funding options need to be explored as angels and HNIs continue to play a key role in early-stage funding. Thereafter, VC players including Indian funds, global funds, funds of large tech and other conglomerates as well as family offices play a key role in funding Series A and Series B rounds,” says Raja Lahiri, Partner, Grant Thornton Bharat. Equity funding led by Angels and VCs continues to be the most popular options primarily because of their high-risk appetite and ability to come in at stages when the product-market fit is still not achieved and their ability to guide and partner with companies apart from the capital infusion.
        According to Sid Talwar, Co-Founder & Partner at Lightbox Ventures, three things have happened in the startup investment ecosystem in India. “The VC community has grown, with both new funds coming in and older funds having a lot more money to deploy; second: the global investor community has grown - from only Chinese and American investors earlier, the community now comprises of people across the world; third: the family office ecosystem has picked up the pace.” Talwar believes that while the ecosystem lacks options and experience, both will come over time.

        For Varun Alagh, Co-Founder, Mamaearth – the only Indian company to cross the Rs 100 crore mark in just four years – the first round of investment came in through friends and family and subsequent fundings through VCs. So, how does one choose a funding option? “Raising equity capital is like getting married, you have to be in that relationship continuously and work together to build a business,” he says and adds, “Apart from the money, the people that you partner with and your equation with them makes a huge difference.” Aarti Gill, Co-founder of OZiva, a plant-based nutrition products brand, agrees and believes that creating a strong business offering and fulfilling the needs of the consumers are critical in the long run. While she opted for debt and internal accruals to start off initially, she raised $5 million from Matrix Partners in 2020. “We are still in the early days of the startup ecosystem, and there is much more to achieve when it comes to taking risks, celebrating failures, investment exits, etc.”
        Tim Draper, American venture capitalist and founder of Draper Fisher Jurvetson, advises that it’s important to understand an investor before approaching for funding. “An investor looks for fit, so if he is backing women, don’t go in with an all-male team. If he does only does technology, don’t bring him a shoe etailer.” An investor also checks market size and competition, so it’s important to go prepared. “Know your market and your competition, and be able to show why your product or service is important in that marketplace,” he adds.
        Apart from debt, equity and VCs, there are other options for companies to raise funds. Alongside mainstream investment funds, there are many successful entrepreneurs who have become angel investors. There are also accelerator programmes for early-stage companies to raise funds and get help in building companies via advice and network. Plus, for companies with a ready playbook who are seeking funding and have poured money into marketing to fuel growth and revenue, there are many venture debt funds that invest without any collateral. Is crowdfunding a viable option? “Yes, it does works. HNIs who do not have a direct network to invest in startups can invest via crowdfunding platforms,” says Akhil Gupta, Founder and Chief Tech & Product Officer, Nobroker.com, the world’s largest C2C real estate platform. Gupta, who himself went through a tough time in 2014 while securing funds for his company, believes things have changed for the better now, with many India/Bharat first companies getting funded more easily. “The ecosystem needs to understand that the success formula for any startup resides in what impact it is capable of creating rather than whether it has been tried and tested already in some part of the world,” he says.

        US judge removes China's Xiaomi from Trump-era blacklist

        AFP | Mar 13, 2021, 10:36 IST

        TimesPoints

        80
        0 Added

        Visit TOI daily & earn TimesPoints!

        Xiaomi was one of nine firms classified by the Pentagon as 'Communist Chinese military companies'. (Representa...Read More
        WASHINGTON: A US judge on Friday ordered Chinese smartphone maker Xiaomi temporarily removed from a government blacklist barring American investment in the company.
        Six days before Donald Trump left office last year, his administration cemented its trade war legacy against Beijing with a series of announcements targeting Chinese firms including Xiaomi, state oil giant CNOOC, and social media darling TikTok.
        Xiaomi was one of nine firms classified by the Pentagon as "Communist Chinese military companies."
        But US District Judge Rudolph Contreras in Washington ruled Friday the departments of defense and treasury "have not made the case that the national security interests at stake here are compelling."
        He issued a preliminary injunction removing Xiaomi from the blacklist and suspending the ban on US investors buying the company's securities.
        In an appeal filed in January against its blacklisting, Xiaomi -- which overtook Apple last year to become the world's third-largest smartphone manufacturer -- said Washington's moves were "incorrect" and had "deprived the company of legal due process."
        Contreras's decision came the same day US regulators listed Huawei and ZTE among Chinese telecom gear firms deemed a threat to national security, signalling that a hoped-for softening of relations is not on the cards.
        Huawei chief and founder Ren Zhengfei last month called for a reset with the United States under President Joe Biden, after the firm was battered by sanctions imposed by Trump's administration.
        The telecoms giant has been at the center of the Sino-American rivalry in recent years, against a backdrop of a trade and technology war between the superpowers.
        Washington claims Huawei has close ties to China's military and that Beijing could use its equipment for espionage -- accusations the company denies.
